Consider the model $$d X_t = \alpha \left(\theta - X_t\right)dt + \sigma dB_t $$ By Ito Isometry, we have: $$\langle X \rangle_t = E_t(X) = \theta \left(1 - e^{-\alpha t} \right) + X_0 e^{-\alpha t}$$ and $$\langle X^2 \rangle_t - \langle X \rangle_t^2 = Vt(X) = \frac{\sigma^2}{2 \alpha }\left(1 - e^{-2 \alpha t} \right)$$ If we assume discrete, uniform sampling of spacing $\tau$, then we have, $$P(\theta | X; \alpha, \sigma) = \sqrt{\frac{1}{2\pi V{\tau} }}^{T-1} \exp\left(-\frac{\sumt^{T-1} \left(X{t+1} - E\right)^2 }{2V}\right) $$

Consider the model $$ X_{t+1} = X_t r e^{-\beta X_t + \sigma Z_t } $$ with $Z_t$ a unit normal random variable. The likelihood of the sequence of $T$ observations of $X$ under this model is thus $$P(X | r, \beta, \sigma) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi \sigma^2}^{T-1}} \exp\left(\frac{\sumt^{T-1} \left(\log X{t+1} - \log X_t - \log r + \beta X_t\right)^2 }{2 \sigma^2}\right) $$ To integrate out $r$, $P(X | \beta, \sigma) = \int P(X | r, \beta, \sigma ) P® dr$, we’ll make this look like a Gaussian in $\log r$ by completing the square; getting the square on the outside of the sum.

I have often observed (e.g. Boettiger & Hastings (2012) ) that if purely stochastic transitions out of a potential well happen, then they happen fast (dispite the fact that waiting time for them to happen is very long). Yesterday Marc mentioned an interesting proof of this from Don Ludwig, who was able to show that the waiting time to reach a large deviation in a time $t$, given that it reaches it at all, is of the same order as the return time from the large deviation to the equilibrium, $\log(L/\sigma)$.
