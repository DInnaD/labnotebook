Exact Simulation by the Gillespie Algorithm

Wed, Jul 6, 2011

The Gillespie algorithm has two parts: (1) determine what event happens, (2) determine when it happens. Consider the system of equations $$ \dot X_i = f_i(\vec X) $$ where the $f_i$ give the rates of creation and annihilation (negative and postive parts of $f_i(\vec X)$ must be listed seperately) for all species $x_i$. We assume this gives the mean rate at which each event occurs, drawn from on exponential distribution. We could simply draw the associated random exponential time for each reaction, and pick the minimum for which event occurs.
