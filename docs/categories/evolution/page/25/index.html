Adaptive Dynamics

Tue, May 4, 2010

Wide gap, flat landscape Waiting times on a flatter landscape: sigma_c ~ sigma_k. Waiting times are dominated by the number of times phase 3 collapses: the established invader in a dimorphic population (right panels). Collapse of phase 2: collapse of the dimorphism before third population can establish is much rarer (about an order of magnitude). from the run3 dataset, with parameters. Narrower gap, steeper landscape with sigma_c << sigma_k, branching is easy, neither phase is particularly limiting, though single collapses from both phases are very common.

Adaptive Dynamics

Mon, Apr 26, 2010

Matching theoretical approximations to simulation: Implemented the first analytic approximation, calculating the waiting time to complete phase 1. Established dimophisms are often still lost, hence the first successful completion of phase 1 (dimorphic population in coexistence region with each above threshold size) doesn’t match the time of the last completion of phase 1. Phase 2, an established third population seems to happen soon after the last successful phase 1, giving the illusion this step is easy rather than the reality that this process requires fast traversal to persist past the coexistence time.
