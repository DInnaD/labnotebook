The Ergodicity Question- Time averaging vs Ensemble averaging

11am – 3pm Tried time averaging over the entire time window (set sample_size to 1 less than max time); in close agreement with the ensemble predictions (getting around 1355). Mystery doesn’t seem to actually be a problem in the method, but just innate variability! This variation around the warning signal/leading indicator is exactly what might frustrate the warning signal.

Towards a meaningful warning signal metric

Most papers do not give a statistical approach, but merely argue for the existence of a trend. Kendall’s tau approach proved very unreliable. While the preliminary de-trending approach is unjustified in that the simulated data is known to be absent from trends, as a kind of windowed averaging it may sense as a way to detect changes. Should distinguish between the gradual slow changes and change-point estimation. Perhaps change-point provides a more rigorous starting point.
