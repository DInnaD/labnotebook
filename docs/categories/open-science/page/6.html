Wrong picture, wrong model: why warning signals don't look like rare events

Mon, Oct 25, 2010

Detecting a warning signal of collapse isn’t about detecting that a system is non-stationary, but more specifically, that the system is about to do something sudden. The classic saddle-node bifurcation example results in a picture like this: A visible steady decrease interrupted by a sudden jump. The goal is to distinguish this from the case of a steady decrease that involves no loss of stability – such as would be created by continually lowering the carrying capacity of logistic-model system at a linear rate.
